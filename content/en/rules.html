RULES: General rules

  1. By accepting an invitation to the convention, you automatically agree with the rules acting during the duration of the convention and across its venue.
  2. To ensure general safety, attendees and their personal belongings may be searched by the police and the event's own security. Should an attendee refuse to undertake the procedure, the organizers may decline them attendance to the event.
  3. The organizers, at their sole discretion, may decline attendance to the event, in accordance with law enforcement and emergency services requirements listed in the Law on gatherings of large groups of people, regardless of the person in question having a ticket.
  4. Children under 14 years attending the event must be accompanied by an adult. (The adult must provide their contact information in case of emergency).
  5. The organizers reserve the right to request any attendee to present an ID confirming their age (a passport or a driver's license).
  6. Attendees and guests of the event agree that any photos and videos made by photographers and cameramen from the organizers' team may be used by the organizers to promote future events (publication on websites, in social media, in presentations, in printed promotional materials, for use by the media).
  7. The organizers are not responsible for attendees' personal belongings.
  8. Any commercial use of the convention's logo (sales) is forbidden.
  9. The organizers reserve the right to decline admission at their sole discretion, even if the person in question carries an invitation.

Admission system

At the entrance, all guests receive (and attach to their wrist) a special bracelet you have to wear until the end of the convention. Loss or taking off the bracelet is a violation of convention rules, and the violator will be removed from the premises. The bracelet has to be worn during whole duration of the convention. If lost, the bracelet can not be redeemed. Passing over the bracelet to other persons is forbidden.

Children at the age of 12 or younger may attend the convention free of charge, if the accompanying adult has a ticket. Only one child may be admitted for free per accompanying adult.

Food and drinks

Attendees may carry non-alcoholic drinks in transparent plastic bottles of up to 1.5 liters in volume. Any other sorts of drinks, as well as any drinks in glass bottles, are forbidden.

Attendees may carry chocolate, energy bars, cookies, candies and pastries in sealed original packaging, as well as fruits. Rapidly spoiling products and products with strong smells are forbidden.

Helpdesk and Info

  1. The event will have a dedicated helpdesk. You can approach them with any questions.
  2. For general questions and navigation tips, you can ask volunteers as well.
  3. Volunteers shall not answer any organizational questions or comments regarding organization of the event.
  4. For comments and with organizational questions, please contact the organizers directly.
  5. You can get in touch with them at the helpdesk.

Attendees of the event shall not:

  1. Break any fire safety, electrical safety and other regulations applicable for public events: smoke outside the designated zones, litter, disturb other attendees in other ways.
  2. Make photos and videos in staff areas (dressing rooms, service and administration areas, facilities behind the scenes etc.)
  3. "Attend the event while intoxicated. (Severity of ""intoxication"" will be roughly estimated by the security)."
  4. Bring and/or consume alcoholic beverages at the venue.
  5. Bring animals (unless approved by the organizers in advance).
  6. Conduct sales without the organizers' approval.
  7. Leave children unattended.
  8. Enter the stage (unless authorized).
  9. Damage property and the venue.
  10. Attend the event in staining clothes.

Booth rules

  1. All decorations should be prepared beforehand. Only assembling of decorations is allowed on the venue. Booth staff may not conduct any painting jobs on the venue, including adjacent territory.
  2. Materials used for construction should have fire safety certification, if possible.
  3. Size of a booth should be discussed by the main host and the booth zone curator beforehand.
  4. Cleaning up on the stand during the convention is conducted by the booth staff.
  5. Booth plan, decoration designs, list of electrical equipment and booth activity program should be agreed upon with the booth zone curator before Wednesday, March 01, 2017.
  6. The booth should have a poster or leaflets detailing their activity program (if applicable).
  7. If there is electrical equipment to be used in the booth, the list of such equipment and its power capacity (in Watt) must be included in the booth's application before Wednesday, February 15, 2017. Use of any electrical equipment not listed in the application is forbidden.
  8. Maximum number of participants that can be included in application as booth staff is determined by the booth zone curator (no more than 15 people).
  9. Any commercial business shall comply with the law of the Russian Federation.
  10. The organizers are not responsible for actions of any people (excluding the event staff) admitted to the event.

Booth hosts may not:

  • Use open fire and pyrotechnics;
  • Use powered cords without electrical isolation;
  • Use electrical equipment not listed in the application;
  • Use any paints and varnishes during preparations and during the event itself;

Sell, demonstrate or distribute any pornographic and erotic material (so-called R34, clop), as well as any material depicting violence and cruelty (so-called dark content), or any other shock-content.

Use following electrical equipment: smoke machines, microwave ovens (unless previously approved by the organizers), cotton candy and popcorn machines.

Sales of any products outside designated zones (sales and sales/entertainment locations)

Rules regarding weapons

  1. "Attendees may not carry cut and thrust weapons, sharp and dangerous items threating health and safety of others, crafted blades etc., except for replicas, souvenirs and toys compliant with the Russian law; Attendees may not carry pneumatic, paintball, stikeball, hardball weapons and firearms (including traumatic).
  2. Rules regarding bows and crossbows:
    • Bows and crossbows shall not be drawn or aimed at people, whether loaded or not.
    • A costume may include a replica bow with fixed limbs and a rubber bow-string. Such replicas may be drawn when not loaded.
    • If a costume must include a bow (a crossbow), and not a replica, its owner must pledge in writing not to draw it under any circumstances.
    • Arrowheads shall only be made out of soft materials, i.e. rubber.
  3. All cosplayers carrying any weapon as a part of their costume shall carry an ID.
  4. Firing any weapons, as well as unauthorized sparring with weapon replicas on the venue are forbidden.
  5. Character play for cosplayers carrying weapons shall be conducted in a way that prevents them from harming themselves, other people and the property.

Changes to the rules

  1. The organizers reserve the right to change the rules and any additional rules (for cosplayers, booths etc.). Information regarding such changes shall be published on the official website and in official VK.COM groups.
  2. The only efficient and correct version of the rules is the one published at the official website of the convention: http://ponirebrik.ru/rules.php

Responsibility for not complying with the rules:

In case of breaking the rules (unless the conflict is resolved in a peaceful manner) the offender will be removed from the premises.

Offending products are removed from owners upon discovery (to prevent further demonstration and sales) and are returned at the end of the convention.

In case of breaking the rules by a booth (unless the conflict is resolved in a peaceful manner) the booth will be removed from the premises.



The Rules are approved by the Convention Ponfederation.
